Learn — one closed loop

A Nikoli original, and the classic “draw a loop” puzzle.

Connect the dots with horizontal and vertical segments to form a single closed loop that never crosses or branches. The numbers inside the cells are the rule: a cell marked 3 has exactly three of its four edges on the loop; a 0 has none; blank cells can be anything. Get every clue right with one unbroken loop and you’re done.

“
Why it belongs here: Slitherlink rewards reasoning about edges and corners rather than cells. A 0 next to a 3, a 3 in a corner — each is a tiny theorem that forces several segments at once.

Technique — corners and zeros

The 0

A 0 has no loop on any side — cross all four edges off. Any clue touching it loses those shared edges, which often forces its remaining segments.

The 3 in a corner

A 3 in the grid’s corner must use both outer edges. Two adjacent 3s force the segment between them plus the two outer edges. Learn a handful of these patterns and loops unzip quickly.

Click an edge to cycle blank → line → × (ruled-out) → blank. Build until one closed loop satisfies every number.
