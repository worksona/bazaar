Lights Out

Every press flips a lamp and its four neighbours. Put the whole grid to dark — a tiny machine of pure parity, solvable without a single guess.

difficulty · beginner⏱ 5–15 minyou’ll learn parity / toggle logic

Learn — one switch, five lamps

Press any lamp and it toggles, along with the lamps above, below, left, and right.

The board starts with some lamps lit. Each press flips a plus-shaped set of five — the lamp you touch and its orthogonal neighbours (edges and corners just flip fewer). The goal is simple: turn every lamp off at once. It sounds fiddly because each press undoes some of your work — but there’s an order that never fails.

“
Why it belongs here: Lights Out is linear algebra in disguise — every solution is a set of presses over “on/off” arithmetic. Pressing a lamp twice cancels out, so only which lamps you press matters, never the order.
2

Technique — chase the lights

A method that always works: clear top-down, then fix the last row.

The chase

Go row by row from the top. Whenever a lamp in the row above your current row is lit, press the lamp directly below it — that’s the only way left to turn it off. Sweep downward and every row clears except possibly the bottom one.

The finish

If lamps remain on the bottom row, the pattern there tells you which lamps in the top row to press; chase again, and the board goes dark. (For this 5×5 there’s always a press-set that finishes it.) Or just experiment — it’s small enough to feel your way through.

3

Solve — douse every lamp

Click lamps until the whole grid is dark. The flag lights up when it does.
