Learn — three rules for two symbols

Also called Takuzu or Binary Puzzle. Every cell is a 0 or a 1.

Fill every cell so that: (1) no three of the same digit sit in a row, across or down — 0 0 0 and 1 1 1 are forbidden; (2) each row and each column holds equal numbers of 0s and 1s; and (3) no two rows are identical, and no two columns are identical.

“
Why it belongs here: with only two symbols, the deductions are crisp and chainable — a pair forces a third cell, a near-full line forces the rest. Like Sudoku stripped to its logical bones.
2

Technique — the forcing moves

Three patterns crack almost every cell.

The pair

Two equal digits side by side force the opposite on both ends: 1 1 _ must be 1 1 0, and _ 1 1 _ becomes 0 1 1 0.

The gap

A digit with a gap then the same digit forces the middle: 1 _ 1 must be 1 0 1, or you’d make a triple later.

The count

Each line needs exactly half 0s and half 1s. Once a row has all its 1s placed, every remaining cell is a 0 — and vice versa.
