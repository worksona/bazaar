Learn — one of each, three ways

Curated into its modern form by Nikoli, the house behind this whole wing.

Fill the 9×9 grid so that every row, every column, and every 3×3 box contains the digits 1–9 exactly once. The givens never change; you place the rest. There’s precisely one solution, reachable by pure elimination — no guessing, ever.

Why it belongs here: Sudoku is the purest constraint-satisfaction puzzle in print — the same logic a SAT solver runs, done by hand. Master the eliminations here and Binairo, Kakuro, and the rest read like dialects of one language.
2

Technique — narrow, then place

Two ideas solve most grids; the rest is patience.

The naked single

For any empty cell, cross off every digit already in its row, column, or box. If only one digit survives, it must go there.

The hidden single

Look at a single row, column, or box. If a digit can legally land in only one of its cells, place it there — even if that cell could also take other digits.

Click a cell, then a number (or press 1–9; Backspace clears). Conflicts turn red; matching digits highlight so you can scan.
