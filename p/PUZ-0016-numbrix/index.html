Numbrix

A single snaking path of consecutive numbers fills the grid. Some are given; thread 1 to 25 so each number touches the next, left-right or up-down.

Learn — follow the thread

Devised by Marilyn vos Savant; a cousin of Hidato.

The grid hides one continuous path numbered 1, 2, 3 … up to 25. Consecutive numbers always sit in orthogonally adjacent cells — never diagonally. A handful of numbers are printed; your job is to fill the rest so the whole sequence joins up into one unbroken walk through every cell.

“
Why it belongs here: every gap between two givens is a little routing problem — count the squares between them and the path almost draws itself. It’s deduction as connect-the-dots.
2

Technique — bridge the givens

Mind the gap

Two givens that differ by k must be joined by a path of exactly k steps. If they’re already k cells apart by the shortest route, that route is forced — fill it straight in.

Work both ends

Extend from 1 forward and from 25 backward at once; they meet in the middle. Cells with only one free neighbour force their successor immediately.

Click an empty cell and type its number (1–25). Backspace clears.

Solve — complete the path

Fill 1 to 25 with every step adjacent. Finish it and the flag lights.
