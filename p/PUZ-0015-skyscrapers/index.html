Skyscrapers

A Latin square of building heights. The number outside each row and column says how many buildings you can see from there — taller ones hide the shorter ones behind them.

Learn — how many can you see

Fill the grid 1–5 so each digit appears once per row and column.

Each cell is a tower of height 1 to 5, and like Sudoku no height repeats in a row or column. The clues sit outside the grid: a clue is the number of towers visible looking in along that line. A taller tower hides every shorter one behind it, so looking past a 5 you see nothing else — a clue of 1 means the nearest tower is the tallest (a 5), and a clue of 5 means they ascend 1·2·3·4·5.

Why it belongs here: the visibility clues turn a plain Latin square into a chain of forced placements — the big clues pin the extremes, and the rest falls out.
2

Technique — the telling clues

The 1 and the N

A clue of 1 forces the tallest tower (5) right against it. A clue of 5 forces the whole line to climb 1·2·3·4·5 in order. Place those first.

Count the hides

For middling clues, ask how many towers must be hidden. A clue of 2 means exactly one tower hides behind a taller one nearer the edge — often only one arrangement fits once a few cells are known.
