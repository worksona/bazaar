What this is

historical cryptography you learn by doing, not by reading

Most explanations of classic ciphers hand you a diagram and wish you luck. The Bazaar hands you the machine. Every puzzle is a small interactive workbench: encode and decode live, watch the mechanism move, then turn the same tools on a real challenge and break it yourself. You leave actually understanding how a famous code worked — and why it eventually fell.

Why use it

for the curious, the puzzle-minded, and anyone learning security or history

Best used like this

  • Give it ~20–30 minutes and read top to bottom — the stations are a path, not a menu.
  • Actually type things in. The engine is there to be poked; intuition comes from watching it move.
  • Climb from the gentlest grade up. Start with Substitution and follow the ladder below; each cipher sets up the next.
  • Pen optional. Everything’s in the browser, but a scrap of paper helps when you’re recovering a keyword by hand.
